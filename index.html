About Me

Born and raised in Queens, New York, I grew up surrounded by the vibrant diversity and creativity of the city, which inspired my love for culture, the arts, and exploration. My journey into computer science began with a high school internship at BridgeUP: STEM, where I discovered the power of coding to transform raw data into meaningful insights, from mapping COVID-19 trends to exploring spider proteins. This experience sparked my passion for leveraging technology to solve complex challenges and create a positive impact. Today, I am driven by the intersection of innovation and human impact, aiming to build software solutions that improve lives while fostering collaboration and inclusivity.

BridgeUP: STEM


As a software engineering intern, I assisted entomology scientists by creating a Python script to identify the largest repeating protein sequence in spider DNA and visualized the evolutionary relationships between spider species using HTML and CSS. Collaborating with my team, we learned biological concepts and discussed approaches to develop a robust tool to support their research.
